The Ultimate Guide to Growing Tomatoes on Your Balcony

No garden? No problem. Learn how to grow juicy, organic tomatoes right outside your apartment door.

Sameh mohamed Master Gardener

Growing your own food is incredibly rewarding, and tomatoes are the perfect gateway plant for balcony gardeners. With the right setup, a few square feet of sunny space is all you need for a summer harvest.

1. Choose the Right Tomato Variety

Not all tomatoes are suited for container life. When shopping for seeds or seedlings, look for Determinate or Bush varieties. These plants grow to a compact, fixed height and produce all their fruit at once.

  • Cherry Tomatoes: Tiny Tim, Sweet Million, or Sungold are incredibly prolific and do beautifully in pots.
  • Patio Tomatoes: Specifically bred for small spaces, like the 'Patio Princess'.

2. Pick the Perfect Pot

Tomatoes have deep root systems and are thirsty plants. The biggest mistake beginners make is choosing a pot that is too small.

You need a container that holds at least 5 to 10 gallons of soil (about 12-14 inches deep and wide). Ensure the pot has plenty of drainage holes at the bottom to prevent root rot.

Pro Tip: Fabric Grow Bags

Consider using fabric grow bags. They are lightweight, perfect for balconies, and allow the roots to breathe, preventing them from becoming root-bound.

3. Soil and Fertilizer are Everything

Never use heavy garden soil in containers; it won't drain properly. Instead, invest in a high-quality potting mix that contains peat moss or coco coir, perlite, and compost.

Tomatoes are heavy feeders. Mix a slow-release organic tomato fertilizer into the soil when planting, and supplement with a liquid fertilizer every two weeks once blossoms appear.

4. Chase the Sun

Tomatoes are sun-worshippers. They require a minimum of 6 to 8 hours of direct sunlight daily to produce sweet, juicy fruit. Observe your balcony's light patterns before placing your pots. South or West-facing balconies usually work best.

5. Consistent Watering is Key

Container soil dries out much faster than ground soil, especially on a breezy balcony. In the peak of summer, you may need to water your plants every single day.

Stick your finger about an inch into the soil; if it feels dry, it’s time to water. Water at the base of the plant to keep the leaves dry, which helps prevent fungal diseases.

6. Provide Support Early

Even compact tomato varieties need support once they are loaded with heavy fruit. Insert a tomato cage or sturdy bamboo stakes into the pot at the time of planting. Doing this later can damage the established root system.
